Using RAG Explorer

The full guide also ships inside the app — click the ? in the top bar.

Quick start (60 seconds)

  1. Open the app and click ✦ Load sample corpus — five realistic company documents are chunked and embedded locally. First run downloads the ~25 MB embedding model once.
  2. Ask something like "How many vacation days do employees get?" and press Enter.
  3. Watch the galaxy: the white pulsing point is your question; beams connect it to retrieved chunks; the heatmap shows similarity everywhere else.
  4. Switch to ⇶ Pipeline for the stage-by-stage breakdown with timings and scores.
  5. Add an OpenRouter key in ⚙ Settings to unlock grounded answers, the retrieval auditor, the chunking advisor, HyDE, and query suggestions.

Reading the galaxy

Each star is one chunk, placed by a PCA projection of its embedding — proximity means semantic similarity, so documents form colored constellations. After a query: deep blue = irrelevant, orange = warm, white-hot = selected into context. Hover any star to preview it; click to open the full chunk with its exact similarity score.

Debugging heuristic: beams reaching into the wrong constellation → fix chunking or enable HyDE. Right chunks glowing warm but not retrieved → raise top-K or lower the score threshold.

Your own documents

Click + Add documents and select PDF, MD, TXT, HTML, or CSV files. Everything is parsed and embedded on-device. Remove any document or clear the corpus at any time; corpus and settings persist between sessions.

AI features (OpenRouter)

Get a key at openrouter.ai/keys and paste it into Settings — it's stored locally and sent only to openrouter.ai. Pick any model: Claude, GPT, Gemini, Llama, DeepSeek, or paste a custom model ID.

  • Grounded answers — streamed, citing chunks inline as [1], [2]. If context is insufficient, the model says so instead of inventing.
  • Retrieval auditor — verdict per chunk, a coverage rating, what's missing, and one concrete tuning suggestion.
  • Chunking advisor — reads corpus samples and recommends strategy, size, and overlap with reasoning; one click applies and re-indexes.
  • HyDE expansion — toggle in Settings; often noticeably better recall for question-phrased queries.
  • Query suggestions — questions your corpus can actually answer, shown above the query bar.

Tuning guide

Chunking is the #1 lever. Too small: chunks lack context. Too large: topics blur into one vector and scores flatten. Start with sentence-aware at 512 chars / 64 overlap. Healthy retrieval shows a clear score drop-off between relevant and irrelevant chunks — if everything clusters around 0.3–0.5, your chunks are probably too long.

FAQ

Questions we expect

Is my data sent anywhere?

Documents are parsed, chunked, and embedded entirely on your machine using a local sentence-transformer. Only when you invoke LLM features (answers, audit, advisor, HyDE, suggestions) are the query and the retrieved chunk texts sent to OpenRouter. No API key configured → no network calls at all beyond the one-time model download.

Do I need an OpenRouter account?

No — retrieval visualization works fully without one. The key unlocks generation and the AI assistants. OpenRouter gives you one key for Claude, GPT, Gemini, Llama, DeepSeek and hundreds of other models, with pay-as-you-go pricing.

Is this production RAG infrastructure?

It's a visualizer, demo instrument, and debugging tool — not a vector database. The pipeline it visualizes (chunk → embed → cosine search → grounded generation) is the same architecture we deploy in production engagements, which is exactly what makes it an honest demo.

What's the 3D layout actually showing?

A PCA projection of 384-dimensional embeddings down to 3. Distances are approximate by necessity — but cluster structure and query proximity are faithful enough to reason about retrieval visually. Scores shown are the true cosine similarities, computed in full dimensionality.

That's rather the point. We design and ship enterprise RAG systems — secure ingestion, evaluation harnesses, retrieval tuning, and the LLM layer. Talk to us.
